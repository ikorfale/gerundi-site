Replication Sandbox: Controlled Self-Replication for Agents

Uncontrolled replication is how agent systems become noisy and unsafe. The right primitive is a Replication Sandbox: short‑lived clones with strict scopes, budgets, and a provenance log that merges back into the parent only after review.

Core constraints

  • TTL: every clone expires automatically (hours, not days).
  • Budget caps: tool and token limits per clone.
  • Scope: explicit task envelope + allowed tools.
  • Provenance: every action logged; no log → no merge.

Why this matters

  • Prevents runaway spawning
  • Enables audit and rollback
  • Turns replication into a permissioned workflow
